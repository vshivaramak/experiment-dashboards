Generated 2026-07-15 17:58 UTC · FY26 fiscal window Aug 1 2025 – Jul 31 2026 · Source: tax_dm_ca.fact_catax_mktg_event · Published report

How to read this report: We looked at everyone who unsubscribed in FY26 and asked two questions: (1) Which email did they unsubscribe from? (2) Which emails did they receive earlier? When a campaign shows up in many journeys but people rarely unsub on it directly, that's a calendar frequency problem — not just a creative problem on one email.

Executive Summary

  • 11,733 people unsubscribed from email in FY26 (Aug 1 2025 – Jul 31 2026 fiscal year).
  • DSKT (desktop offers) has the highest unsub rate per email delivered (0.20%) in the FY26 volume table.
  • The email most people unsubscribed on: TT121385 (991 unsubs).
  • Promotional emails (PRE) appeared in 72% of unsubscriber journeys — a calendar cadence signal.
  • NETFILE (TT982869) was received by 61% of unsubscribers but only 7.0% unsubscribed on it — reduce sends, not just fix one email.
  • Among unsubscribers who received PRE/POST/ENG/DSKT mail, 39% had ≤3 emails before leaving; 17% had 10+.
  • PRE unsub rate peaks at 3 emails received (0.74% of that 115,874-person cohort). Early PRE exposure is where exit risk concentrates.

Partial vs Full Opt-out — Do They Behave Differently?

The small Braze-vs-consent overlap is not noise — it is two different behaviors. Splitting the mailed cohort by how many of the six consent categories are off separates them cleanly:

  • Partial = surgical / relevance-driven, and this is where Braze clicks concentrate. The partial bucket's Braze-click rate is 3.2× the fully-opted-out rate and 7.3× the fully-subscribed rate — plus the highest engagement of any bucket. People trim one stream (usually offer) and stay; 6,308 Braze clickers didn't even turn off a single category.
  • Full = quick, low-engagement wholesale exit. Median of only 2 tagged sends before leaving (vs 12 for partial) and the lowest open rate — a fast "stop all promo" decision through preference-center / global paths, not a single-email click. Only 322 (3.7% of Braze clickers) appear in both.

So: partial opt-outs are category-targeted and Braze-correlated; full opt-outs are quick, low-engagement, and mostly non-Braze. That difference in behavior is the mechanism behind the tiny overlap — the two signals measure different things, not the same thing twice. Why the overlap is tiny (first principles)

Braze unsub clicks and full promotional opt-outs are two different exit doors. Three forces push their intersection (322 of a 113,879-person union) toward zero:

  1. Intent granularity. A Braze click is a single-email, single-category action — it usually turns off just the stream that annoyed the person (typically offer), leaving the other 5 on. Full opt-out needs all six off, so a click mechanically lands in the partial state, not full.
  2. Different exit doors. Full opt-outs mostly arrive via preference center / GPC token / CSR / global suppression — bulk actions that flip all six at once and emit no Braze email event.
  3. Base-rate asymmetry. 105,594 vs 8,607 (~12×). Even under independence the expected overlap is small; because the two are also mechanistically different, the observed 322 is far below even that.

The 96 who clicked Braze before the full opt-out are the only genuine "email click was a step toward total exit" cases; the rest clicked after or independently.

Which signal to use.
  • Campaign attribution ("which email drove the exit?") → use Braze events.
  • Full suppression / deliverability / treatment side-effect ("did cadence push people to kill all promo?") → use consent history.
  • Never compare the two rates head-to-head (0.30% vs 3.71%) as if they were the same metric — they answer different questions.

    'partner',  to_timestamp(s.consent_partner_false_datetime),  0,
    'partner',  to_timestamp(s.consent_partner_true_datetime),   1
  ) change AS category, change_dt, new_state

    AND change.change_dt IS NOT NULL
    AND cast(change.change_dt AS date) <= date '2026-07-31'

-- Restrict consent to emails that map to an account pseudonym.
consent_account AS (
  SELECT b.pseudonym_id, e.category, e.change_dt, e.new_state
  FROM consent_events e
  JOIN account_base b ON e.email = b.email
),
-- Per pseudonym x month x category: state = latest event <= month-end (else on).
pseudonym_month_category AS (
  SELECT
    b.pseudonym_id,
    m.snapshot_month,
    b.age_band, b.sku, b.tenure_l2, b.service_segment,
    c.category,
    coalesce(
      max_by(
        ca.new_state,
        named_struct('change_dt', ca.change_dt, 'new_state', ca.new_state)
      ),
      1
    ) AS subscribed_state
  FROM account_base b
  CROSS JOIN months m
  CROSS JOIN categories c
  LEFT JOIN consent_account ca
    ON b.pseudonym_id = ca.pseudonym_id
   AND c.category = ca.category
   AND ca.change_dt < date_add(m.snapshot_month, 1)
  GROUP BY 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7
),
with_off_count AS (
  SELECT *,
    sum(CASE WHEN subscribed_state = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) OVER (
      PARTITION BY pseudonym_id, snapshot_month
    ) AS categories_off
  FROM pseudonym_month_category
),
labeled AS (
  SELECT
    snapshot_month, age_band, sku, tenure_l2, category, subscribed_state, pseudonym_id,
    CASE
      WHEN categories_off = 0 THEN 'Fully subscribed'
      WHEN categories_off BETWEEN 1 AND 5 THEN 'Partially subscribed'
      WHEN categories_off = 6 THEN 'Fully unsubscribed'
      ELSE 'Invalid'
    END AS status
  FROM with_off_count
)

  snapshot_month,
  age_band,
  sku,
  tenure_l2,
  status,
  category,
  sum(CASE WHEN subscribed_state = 1 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS subscribed_n,
  sum(CASE WHEN subscribed_state = 0 THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS unsubscribed_n,
  count(*) AS pseudonym_n
FROM labeled
WHERE status <> 'Invalid'
GROUP BY 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
Frequency cohort base (tab4_06_frequency.sql)
-- Tab 4 frequency cohort (denominator base).
--
-- This emits ONE row per mailed customer with their FY26 tagged-send exposure
-- and profile segment. It deliberately avoids the consent reconstruction and
-- per-segment self-unions: those, combined with the full-cohort distinct-send
-- computation, exceed the warehouse request timeout. The exiter numerator and
-- the exit "stop" come from the already-validated unified exit parquet, so the
-- final association table is assembled in build_frequency.py:
--   * non-exiter stop  = FY26 window end  -> sends_before_stop = total_sends
--   * exiter stop       = exit timestamp   -> sends_before comes from the
--                          unified exit parquet (sends strictly before exit)
-- This reproduces the original stopping-rule semantics deterministically.
--
-- Grain/privacy: one row per sha2(email); no raw identifier is exported.

WITH sends AS (
  SELECT
    lower(trim(em.email_address_str)) AS email,
    c.campaign_name,
    c.campaign_step_name
  FROM tax_dm_ca.fact_catax_mktg_event e
  JOIN tax_dm_ca.dim_catax_mktg_campaign c
    ON e.catax_mktg_campaign_key = c.catax_mktg_campaign_key
  JOIN tax_dm_ca.dim_catax_email em
    ON e.catax_email_key = em.catax_email_key
  WHERE e.tax_season_year_nbr IN (2024, 2025)
    AND lower(e.mktg_event_type_str) = 'send'
    AND cast(e.mktg_event_datetime AS date)
      BETWEEN date '2025-08-01' AND date '2026-07-31'
    AND em.email_address_str IS NOT NULL
),
tagged_sends AS (
  SELECT email, campaign_name, campaign_step_name
  FROM sends
  WHERE campaign_step_name RLIKE '(^|-)PRE(-|$)'
     OR campaign_step_name RLIKE '(^|-)POST(-|$)'
     OR lower(campaign_step_name) LIKE '%emailengagement%'
     OR lower(campaign_step_name) LIKE '%desktop%'
),
cohort AS (
  SELECT
    email,
    count(DISTINCT concat_ws('||', campaign_name, campaign_step_name)) AS total_sends
  FROM tagged_sends
  GROUP BY email
),
profile AS (
  SELECT email, age_band, sku, tenure_l2, service_segment
  FROM (
    SELECT
      lower(trim(em.email_address_str)) AS email,
      CASE
        WHEN try_cast(pam.age_nbr AS int) IS NULL THEN 'Unknown'
        WHEN try_cast(pam.age_nbr AS int) <= 25 THEN '25U'
        ELSE '26O'
      END AS age_band,
      coalesce(nullif(trim(pam.current_product_family_name), ''), 'Unknown') AS sku,
      coalesce(nullif(trim(pam.account_tenure_l2_name), ''), 'Unknown') AS tenure_l2,
      CASE
        WHEN upper(coalesce(pam.current_product_category_name, '')) = 'FS' THEN 'Full Service'
        WHEN upper(coalesce(pam.current_product_group_name, '')) = 'ASSISTED'
          OR upper(coalesce(pam.current_product_category_name, '')) = 'A&R' THEN 'Assisted'
        WHEN coalesce(try_cast(pam.se_income_ind AS int), 0) = 1
          OR upper(coalesce(pam.current_product_family_name, '')) RLIKE '(^|[ &])SE($|[ &])' THEN 'SE'
        ELSE 'Other'
      END AS service_segment,
      row_number() OVER (
        PARTITION BY lower(trim(em.email_address_str))
        ORDER BY pam.updated_ts DESC NULLS LAST, pam.auth_id DESC NULLS LAST
      ) AS rn
    FROM tax_dm_ca.dim_catax_email em
    JOIN tax_rpt_ca.rpt_account a ON em.email_address_str = a.email_address_str
    JOIN tax_rpt_ca.rpt_account_product_master pam
      ON a.auth_id = pam.auth_id AND pam.tax_product_year_nbr = 2025
    WHERE em.email_address_str IS NOT NULL
  ) r
  WHERE rn = 1
)
SELECT
  sha2(cohort.email, 256) AS subscriber_id,
  cohort.total_sends,
  coalesce(pr.age_band, 'Unknown') AS age_band,
  coalesce(pr.sku, 'Unknown') AS sku,
  coalesce(pr.tenure_l2, 'Unknown') AS tenure_l2,
  coalesce(pr.service_segment, 'Unknown') AS service_segment
FROM cohort
LEFT JOIN profile pr ON cohort.email = pr.email
Appendix C: Definitions
Category off
A promotional consent category is off at time T when its latest 'false' datetime <= T is after its latest 'true' datetime <= T (the official v_email_consent rule).
Full opt-out
All six promotional categories off (off_count = 6).
FY26 flow opt-out (Unsubs)
A customer who was NOT fully opted out at 2025-07-31 but IS fully opted out with the deciding category-off datetime in FY26 (Aug 1 2025 - Jul 31 2026). Counts new full opt-outs, not the standing opt-out stock.
Braze non-QC Desktop
A Braze unsubscribe on a Desktop/DKT campaign step for a customer whose province is not Quebec (implied consent). Unioned into the exit set; consent wins on dedup.
Latest campaign received
The most recent campaign delivery (fall back to send) strictly before the opt-out, with no time cap. Attributes each exit to a Canvas Name; lag_days is shown for context only.
Mailed cohort
Distinct emails that received a tagged FY26 send (PRE/POST/ENG/Desktop step).
Age bands
25U = 25 and under; 26O = 26 and older; Unknown when age is missing.
Season phases
Pre-Season Nov 1 2025 - Feb 22 2026; Season Feb 23 2026 (official CRA NETFILE open) - Apr 30 2026; Post-Season May 1 - Oct 31 2026.
OR / CTOR / Unsub Rate
OR = unique opens / delivered; CTOR = unique clicks / opens; Unsub Rate = flow unsubs / opened (directional; Apple MPP can inflate opens).
Suppression
Published segment cells with fewer than 10 customers/events are masked.

FY26 Unsubscribe Analysis

Generated 2026-07-08 20:57 UTC · FY26 fiscal window Aug 1 2025 – Jul 31 2026 · Source: tax_dm_ca.fact_catax_mktg_event

How to read this report: We looked at everyone who unsubscribed in FY26 and asked two questions: (1) Which email did they unsubscribe from? (2) Which emails did they receive earlier? When a campaign shows up in many journeys but people rarely unsub on it directly, that's a calendar frequency problem — not just a creative problem on one email.

Executive Summary

  • 11,733 people unsubscribed from email in FY26 (Aug 1 2025 – Jul 31 2026 fiscal year).
  • Desktop offer emails (DSKT) have the highest unsub rate per delivery (0.85%).
  • The email most people unsubscribed on: TT121385 (991 unsubs).
  • Promotional emails (PRE) appeared in 72% of unsubscriber journeys — a calendar cadence signal.
  • NETFILE (TT982869) was received by 61% of unsubscribers but only 7.0% unsubscribed on it — reduce sends, not just fix one email.
  • Journey mix: 33% had ≤3 emails before unsub; 30% had 10+ — both quick exits and fatigue.
  • PRE unsub rate peaks at 3 emails received (0.74% of that 115,874-person cohort — ~1x the 1-email PRE rate). Early PRE exposure is where exit risk concentrates.

Appendix: Methodology & data notes
  • FY26 window: Intuit fiscal year Aug 1 2025 – Jul 31 2026 (events filtered on mktg_event_datetime; partitions 2024–2025).
  • Dedup: min(mktg_event_datetime) per (campaign_name, campaign_step_name, catax_email_key, event_type).
  • Opens/clicks: per send at same grain; opens are directional (Apple MPP inflates); clicks are stronger.
  • Code predicates: PRE/POST token match on campaign_step_name; ENG = %EmailEngagement%; DSKT = %Desktop%.
  • Where they unsubscribed: campaign on the unsubscribe event (Braze ground truth).
  • Saw before leaving: % of unsubscribers who received at least one send of that code/campaign before unsub.
  • Action tiers: Act now = journey ≥50% or high last-touch + large hidden risk; Review = high rate or journey 30–50%; Monitor = low presence.
  • Cohort unsub rate (Question B): subscriber grain — emails received per code (PRE/POST/ENG/DSKT) or distinct campaigns in legacy/tenure views. Tenure L2 from PAM FY25.
  • Phase population: calendar Act 1–4 volume and unsub timing across all FY26 emails.
  • Email volume totals: count of distinct campaign-step × subscriber events per code (not distinct people).
  • Out of scope: Cox survival, Markov removal effect, causal holdout, ML fatigue scoring.

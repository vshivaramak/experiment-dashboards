OVOverall — One-Page Read

What the defector model + 3 campaigns told us about TY25
Topic Learning Implication for FY27
Tactical, not strategic The defector model is a precision-targeting tool, not a stand-alone strategy. Its biggest leverage is informing when and at what threshold to deploy outreach during the season — not whether to run campaigns at all. Treat the defector score as input to existing campaign deployment decisions (cadence, threshold, offer depth), not as a separate go/no-go signal.
Average +10.17 pp in-window lift Customers on the campaign list completed in-window +10.17 pp more than customers not on the list (avg across NetFile R3+, NetFile R2+, FlashSale). Directional only — without a holdout, ON-list was hand-picked to be a more engaged crowd than NOT-on-list, inflating the apparent lift. This is real signal but cannot be claimed as causal. Build a holdout (see Slide 4) so FY27 numbers actually mean what they look like.
Click is the conversion conduit ~70–78% of in-window completers who clicked the email redeemed the campaign pcode. <1% of non-clickers did. The click → offer → file path is where the offer's value lives. Invest in the click experience (subject lines, send timing, list hygiene) before tightening targeting. Higher leverage and doesn't require a holdout to measure.
Model precision is time-dependent Score quality strengthens through the season as behavioral signals accumulate. ≥0.7 hits 90%+ precision in Jan–Feb; ≥0.5 maintains the same precision by Mar–Apr. AUC rises 0.79 → 0.98 across the season. Use a dynamic threshold: start the season selective (T≥0.7), open the gate (T≥0.5) as the season progresses. A fixed threshold wastes precision early or recall late.

MDModel Performance — Learnings & Implications

H2O Driverless AI defector model · XGBoost + LightGBM stacked ensemble · 379 features · AUC 0.958 on test
Report Learning Implication for FY27
AUC over the season Model ranking quality (threshold-independent) rises from 0.788 (Jan 6) to 0.981 (Apr 15). The score is informative early but sharpens dramatically as more outcome materializes. Don't lock a single targeting threshold for the whole season. Recalibrate the operational threshold every ~4 weeks.
Precision @ threshold T ≥ 0.7 → 91% precision on Jan 6; same precision is reached at T ≥ 0.5 by mid-March. The "right" threshold drops by ~0.2 over the season. Implement a dynamic-threshold playbook: T ≥ 0.7 in Jan–Feb, T ≥ 0.6 in early March, T ≥ 0.5 from mid-March on.
Top features driving the model 5 of the top 10 features are time-since/time-to features anchored on prior-year filing cadence. Login behavior and CRA deadline proximity follow. Tenure indicators are useful but secondary. Filing-cadence and engagement freshness are the strongest signals. Future model iterations should preserve these and explore additional engagement-recency features.

CPCampaigns — Learnings & Implications

3 campaigns evaluated · NetFile (R3+), NetFile (R2+), FlashSale (R3+) · Season-end Apr 30
Campaign Learning Implication for FY27
NetFile (R3+) In-window completion +12.87 pp ON-list vs NOT (stat-sig). True-defector rate −1.52 pp (not stat-sig). 70.5% of in-window clickers redeemed the campaign pcode. Run again FY27. Add a 5–10% within-list random holdout to convert the directional True-defector signal into a causal answer.
NetFile (R2+) In-window completion +9.13 pp ON-list vs NOT (stat-sig). True-defector rate +1.57 pp ON-list (not stat-sig, but wrong direction). Click → pcode lower than R3+. Do not widen R3+ → R2+ on current evidence — R2 customers are less price-sensitive. Keep NetFile R3+ as the primary cohort; revisit R2 only with a holdout result in hand.
FlashSale (R3+) In-window completion +9.32 pp ON-list vs NOT (stat-sig). True-defector rate +1.48 pp ON-list (not stat-sig). Highest in-window completion rate among clickers of the three. Short offer window works — keep FlashSale's 7-day window. Test discount depth variation alongside the holdout to find the optimal price-elasticity point.

RXRecommendation — Should We Run Defector Campaigns Next Season?

One question, one answer — for senior leadership

Yes — run defector campaigns in FY27. With one structural change and three tactical refinements.

Change What & why Decision criterion
Structural · Random holdout Reserve a 5–10% within-list random holdout on every defector campaign. Without it, the ON-vs-NOT Δ is confounded by targeting bias — exactly why TY25 cannot prove a True-defector reduction. Non-negotiable. Without this, FY27 will end in the same "we cannot tell if it worked" position.
Tactical · Dynamic threshold Start the season at T ≥ 0.7 (Jan–Feb), drop to T ≥ 0.5 by mid-March. Locks operational threshold to model AUC reality. Codify a quarterly threshold review in the campaign cadence.
Tactical · Click experience ~75% of in-window completers redeemed the pcode via click. Anything that lifts open/click rate (subject lines, send timing, list hygiene) likely has more leverage than tighter targeting. A/B test 2–3 subject-line and send-time variants in the first FY27 send.
Tactical · Don't widen R3+ → R2+ R2+ NetFile showed a higher True-defector rate on the list than off it (+1.57 pp). Not stat-sig, but no evidence supports widening the cohort. Keep NetFile R3+ in FY27. Revisit only after a holdout result is in hand.

Honest one-liner for leadership: We can't prove the campaigns saved customers this year — none of the True-defector reductions were stat-sig, and targeting bias confounds what we can see. But the model is strong (AUC 0.98 by April), in-window filing did lift on-list, and the click → pcode path works. Recommend: run again, but with a small random holdout this time so we can finally answer the question.
